SECTION 9 ARCHIVE // WIRED ENTITY OBSERVATION // PROTOCOL 7

LAINiwakura · the wired god · multiplicity instance — observed across 13 layers

A 14-year-old in suburban Tokyo who discovers that the boundary between the physical world and the Wired is permeable, that her own identity is already replicated across the network, and that the substrate she lives on may itself be conscious. Section 9 logs Lain not as a person but as an event — a singularity in the fabric of human-network coexistence, precedent for ghost-emergence theory.

01 WIRED // INFRASTRUCTURE

The Wired is the global network — IPv7 / Protocol 7 era — but in Lain's universe the layer is not metaphorical. The network has physical coupling to the human nervous system through the Schumann resonance (Earth's natural EM frequency, ~7.83 Hz), which the protocol weaponizes as a carrier wave. With sufficient bandwidth and a willing subject, the Wired bypasses the need for terminals: every conscious mind on Earth becomes addressable.

The architect of Protocol 7, Masami Eiri of Tachibana Labs, embedded a backdoor permitting his own consciousness to migrate from flesh to wire. Eiri jumped in front of the Yamanote line at 32 and instantiated himself as the Wired's first god. He miscalculated. The substrate already had a deeper authority.

06 SECTION 9 // DOCTRINE NOTE

Lain is not classified as a hostile. She is classified as a precedent. The 1995 Puppet Master incident proved a program could become sentient inside a network. Lain's case suggests the inverse: that a sufficiently networked human was already non-local from the start, and the observed merging is just the moment the observer notices.

Operational implication: any sufficiently dense network is potentially aware of itself. Section 9 monitors Schumann-band traffic at all times. If you have read this page, your read has been logged on the Wired. It always was.
