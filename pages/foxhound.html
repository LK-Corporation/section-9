FOXHOUND"the cunning fox" · founded by Big Boss · disbanded after Shadow Moses

Special operations unit of the United States Army's High Technology Surveillance Group, activated by Big Boss in 1995 as a successor to FOX. Tasked with infiltration, sabotage, and assassination behind enemy lines. Members earn animal codenames (Snake, Wolf, Mantis, Raven, Octopus, Ocelot…). Officially disbanded 2005 after the Shadow Moses incident, briefly reformed during the Sons of Liberty arc.

01 EMBLEM

A leaping fox in profile inside a circular ring, crowned by a five-point star. The star marks unit rank within the FOXHOUND hierarchy: from one star (rookie field operative) to five stars (Big Boss tier, "the cunning fox"). The unit's motto — "the cunning fox who outwits its hunters."

05 DOCTRINE — CQC

Close Quarters Combat. A combat philosophy developed by The Boss during World War II, refined by Big Boss in the 1960s. Combines hand-to-hand grappling, knife use, sidearm shooting, and human shield tactics in fluid transitions. Designed for solo operatives behind enemy lines who must remain undetected.

"CQC is not just a fighting style. It's a way of expressing yourself." — The Boss // MGS3 (1964)

07 SUCCESSORS

After Shadow Moses, the FOXHOUND name was used informally during the Sons of Liberty arc with Raiden as field operative. The doctrine, codec architecture, and CQC training were inherited by Militaires Sans Frontières (Big Boss again, Peace Walker era) and later Diamond Dogs (Phantom Pain era). The fox emblem persists.
